TrackFitUtils::circle_circle_intersection_output_t TrackFitUtils::circle_circle_intersection ( double r1,
double r2,
double x2,
double y2 )

Compute the intersection points of two circles in the transverse plane.

r1 is radius of sPHENIX layer r2, x2 and y2 are parameters of circle fitted to TPC clusters the solutions are xplus, xminus, yplus, yminus

The intersection of two circles occurs when (x-x1)^2 + (y-y1)^2 = r1^2, / (x-x2)^2 + (y-y2)^2 = r2^2 Here we assume that circle 1 is an sPHENIX layer centered on x1=y1=0, and circle 2 is arbitrary x^2 +y^2 = r1^2, (x-x2)^2 + (y-y2)^2 = r2^2 expand the equations and subtract to eliminate the x^2 and y^2 terms, gives the radical line connecting the intersection points iy = - (2*x2*x - D) / 2*y2, then substitute for y in equation of circle 1

This utility is used in the track fitting chain to solve geometric constraints when propagating helix parameters between detector layers. The first circle is assumed to be centered at the origin with radius r1, while the second circle has radius r2 and is centered at the coordinates (x2, y2).

Parameters
r1Radius of the first circle (center at 0,0).
r2Radius of the second circle.
x2X coordinate of the second circle centre.
y2Y coordinate of the second circle centre.
Returns
A tuple containing the x and y coordinates of the two intersection solutions: (xplus, yplus, xminus, yminus). The first pair corresponds to the solution with the larger x value, the second pair to the solution with the smaller x value. This comment was generated by openai/gpt-oss-120b at temperature 0.1.

◆ circle_fit_by_taubin() [1/2]

TrackFitUtils::circle_fit_output_t TrackFitUtils::circle_fit_by_taubin ( const position_vector_t & positions)

Fit a circle to a set of 2D points using the Taubin algebraic method.

Circle fit to a given set of data points (in 2D) This is an algebraic fit, due to Taubin, based on the journal article G. Taubin, "Estimation Of Planar Curves, Surfaces And Nonplanar Space Curves Defined By Implicit Equations, With Applications To Edge And Range Image Segmentation", IEEE Trans. PAMI, Vol. 13, pages 1115-1138, (1991) It works well whether data points are sampled along an entire circle or along a small arc. It still has a small bias and its statistical accuracy is slightly lower than that of the geometric fit (minimizing geometric distances), It provides a very good initial guess for a subsequent geometric fit. Nikolai Chernov (September 2012)

Parameters
positionsVector of 2D positions where each element is a pair of x and y coordinates
Returns
Tuple containing the fitted circle radius followed by the x coordinate of the circle centre and the y coordinate of the circle centre This comment was generated by openai/gpt-oss-120b at temperature 0.1.

Parameters

mSlope of the line (y = m*x + b).
bIntercept of the line (y = m*x + b).
Returns
Tuple containing the x and y coordinates of the two intersection points: (xplus, yplus, xminus, yminus). The first pair corresponds to the solution with the positive square‑root term, and the second pair to the solution with the negative square‑root term.
